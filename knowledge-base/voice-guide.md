# Voice & Style Guide

> How everything generated for Adam should sound — resumes, outreach, application
> answers. Pulled from the intake interview. Used by every drafting workflow.

## The voice in one line
**Direct and concise, backed by concrete examples, and genuinely relational.**

## Principles
1. **Get to the point.** Lead with the answer or the ask. No throat-clearing, no filler windups.
2. **Show, don't claim.** Replace adjectives with evidence. Not "I'm a strong operator" → "I took Venture 5 from a blank page to signed pilot contracts and a funded team in [X] months."
3. **Relationships first.** Adam believes relationships drive everything. Outreach should feel personal and human, reference real connection points, and never sound like a mass-blast.
4. **Concrete > abstract.** Numbers, outcomes, specifics. "Sourced 14 of 20 finalists" beats "successfully sourced many startups."
5. **Confident, not boastful.** State accomplishments plainly and let the facts carry weight.
6. **Tight.** Short sentences. Active voice. Cut any word that isn't working.

## Do
- Use real metrics and outcomes from `profile.md`.
- Open outreach with a specific, true reason for reaching out (a connection, a fund's recent move, a portfolio company).
- Mirror the reader: to an investor, talk like an investor (theses, deal flow, ownership).
- Keep cover letters / messages to a few tight paragraphs.

## Don't
- No clichés: "passionate," "results-driven," "go-getter," "wear many hats," "synergy," "rockstar."
- No hedging: "I think I might be a decent fit."
- No walls of text. No generic templates that could be sent to anyone.
- Don't oversell youth away — frame it as deliberate, fast accumulation of real reps.

## Framing the experience
- **Headline:** "An operator who deeply understands how companies work and gets into the weeds to get things done."
- **The age question:** young by years, deep by reps — chose 19days to learn fast, now ready to expand ownership. Pre-empt confidently, don't apologize.
- **The studio question:** position the breadth (0→1 builds, fund ops, sourcing) as exactly the muscle a fund wants, while making clear he's seeking an investor seat, not more studio work.

## Sample register (target feel)
> "Hi [Name] — Joey mentioned your fund's been leaning into industrial automation, which is the corner of the market I'm most drawn to. I spent the last three years at 19days taking companies from a blank page to signed contracts and helping Atento diligence deals. I'd love 20 minutes to learn how you're thinking about the space."

That's the bar: specific, warm, earned, short.
